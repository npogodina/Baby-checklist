Car Seat + Stroller = Travel System

One thing you absolutely have to bring to the birth center is a car seat, and while all infant car seats look very similar, the most convenient would be the one which comes with a travel system. It will include a base which you install in the back seat of your car, a car seat itself and a carrier which the car seat clicks in to. This allow to move your baby in and out of the car without having to wake her up! Absolute must have. The one I chose – Chicco Bravo – also transforms into a regular stroller which will carry your baby from ~6 month (that’s when they start sitting up) through toddler years. Good to know: you can purchase multiple car bases if you use more than one car.

Chicco Bravo Trio Travel System: $379.99, Amazon.com

Alternative: bassinet included

One downside of Chicco Bravo Travel System is that it doesn’t come with a bassinet, which means that if you want to take your baby on a walk, she’ll have to stay in a somewhat upright position strapped to the seat for the whole time. Bassinet, on the opposite side, provides the comfort of the crib and your baby will lay down in it perfectly flat. I personally didn’t enjoy long walks for the first half a year of my baby’s life, so for my needs – such as shopping trips – Chicco Bravo worked just fine. My friend, however, spent hours pushing the stroller at the nearest waterfront park. Well, she was lucky to live near one, and then again that’s how her baby prefferd to sleep. Her family chose to invest into a more functional, more solid and definitely fancier option. The model offers a car seat, a bassinet and a carrier which will work with both of them.

UPPAbaby Vista Stroller & MESA Car Seat Bundle: $1,199.98, Amazon.com

Diaper backpack

Now one might argue that any backpack or roomy bag will work just fine for purposes of carrying diapers and bottles, but hey, I didn’t have a spare one. A diaper backpack will also come with a changing pad and insulated bottle pockets – nice! Not to mention tons of pockets to keep you organized. And there are all kinds of designs for you to choose from. My husband and I decided on a neutral gray one so we both feel good about wearing it.

HapTim Multi-Function Large Baby Diaper Bag: $46.99, Amazon.com

Blanket: keep her warm!

Regardless of what time of the year we’re talking about, it’s a good idea to have a medium warmth blanket handy, even during summer. Because, you know, air conditioners. A blanket is an easy way to add an extra layer to your baby’s outfit, and it’s actually the only safe way to keep your baby warm in a car in colder months. Safety guidelines forbid parents from bundling up little ones in winter coats and parkas when putting them in a car seat, because the straps won’t tighten well enough. You dress the baby into a fleece suit, strap her into the seat and then tuck a blanket on top of it. My family ended up getting a blanket for every car, stroller and place where baby has to spend time at. We had at least five, if not more!

The very important piece here – you can’t leave a baby with a blanket unsupervised because of a suffocation risk. That means that you’re either constantly keeping an eye on him or swaddle/tuck the blanket into the car seat nice and snug.

Hudson Baby Super Plush Blanket: $11.99, Amazon.com

Car window shades

For car shades, if you’re planning on going places with your newborn and your area gets lots of sun (lucky!), then they are pretty much a necessity. My baby was born in Seattle in the midst of raining season, so I was able to postpone the purchase until later. I still had to get them though! And they don’t have to be a special baby product. It’s more important that they fit well in your car (and cover as much window as possible) than what giraffe picture they have on them.

Enovoe Car Window Shade: $12.97, Amazon.com

Baby car mirror: connect & have fun!

A car mirror is more of an optional purchase. It attaches to the back seat so that you can see the baby while driving. To me it was very well worth it, but it’s still not the same as being next to her. My baby girl slept very well in the car and I remember myself wondering if she’s sleeping or… ? Ah, let’s pull to the side and check!.. There are also car mirrors that lit up and play music for your little one’s entertainment. My baby really liked ours when she was ~3-5 months, but it won’t be much fun for an older baby.

Brica® Baby In-Sight® Magical Firefly™ Car Back Seat Mirror $36.99, buybuybaby.com

Crib

Crib is not only a place where your baby sleeps. It is also a safe place where you can leave the baby unattended knowing that she won’t roll over and fall (the danger becomes real at ~4 months of age). Therefore, definitely, get a crib! I am linking the one we ended up buying – and we are very happy with it! – but, honestly, I think the decision should come up to your furniture preferences. Cribs come in two sizes – standard and mini. You need a standard one which will fit a mattress of 28 x 52. Most cribs will feature adjustable mattress heights. Putting a baby down into a crib without waking her up is quite a task, so assemble the crib with your mattress on the highest setting – you’ll be grateful for that!

Now, what you also need to know is that when your baby will start sleeping in the crib is a big question, and will depend on your thoughts around co-sleeping. Co-sleeping is considered unsafe and oh so not recommended by American Academy of Pediatrics, but many Moms end up going co-sleeping route anyways. Sleep training an infant is hard. Having to get up for a feeding 3-4 times/night is hard.

Delta Canton 4-in-1 Convertible Crib: $240.27, Amazon.com

Optional: co-sleeper bassinet

Now many parents are not excited about the crib in the beginning, because it seems too big, too cold and too far away from their bed. There are all kinds of bassinets on the market, including so called bedside bassinets – those which are meant to be positioned right next to your bed for easy checking on the baby. The downside of any bassinet is that the baby will inevitably outgrow it in a next few months and will have to go back into the crib, so for me it felt like an unnecessary expense. I didn’t have a bassinet. My friend – on the contrary – did and she loved it! Their bassinet was designed for bed sharing – they placed it right on their bed between Mom and Dad. She said the baby was much happier sleeping next to them and with the C-section it was a huge relief to be able to take care of him without having to get up.

SwaddleMe By Your Side Sleeper: $45.38, Amazon.com
